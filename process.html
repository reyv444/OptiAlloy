Anodizing Process

Anodizing is a surface treatment mostly used on aluminum to make it stronger, longer lasting, and ready for color or coating. Instead of just painting the metal, anodizing actually changes the metal’s surface at a microscopic level so it becomes part of the metal itself.

Step‑by‑Step Process

Cleaning and Prep

Dirty, oily, or contaminated metal produces bad results. Parts are first cleaned in special chemical baths to remove grease and dirt.

Etching & Desmutting

The surface is lightly etched to create a consistent texture, and any stubborn residues are washed away.

The Anodizing Tank

Clean parts are submerged in a tank filled with an acid solution. Electric current runs through them, causing oxygen to bond with the aluminum surface. A thick layer of aluminum oxide grows that is integrated with the part, not just sitting on top.

Optional Coloring

The anodized surface has tiny pores like microscopic holes that can soak up dye. This is why anodized aluminum can be deeply colored yet still durable.

Sealing

The pores are closed in a hot water or chemical solution, locking in corrosion resistance and preventing color from washing out.

Why It Matters

Anodizing matters because it creates protection that is built into the metal itself rather than sitting on top like paint or plating, which means it will not chip, flake, or peel under normal conditions.

The process forms a durable oxide layer that significantly improves corrosion resistance, allowing aluminum to perform reliably in outdoor and harsh industrial environments for many years compared to untreated material.

This oxide layer is also much harder than raw aluminum, giving the surface better resistance to scratches, friction, and everyday wear.

In addition, the microscopic pores formed during anodizing allow dyes to penetrate beneath the surface, making a wide range of long lasting colors possible although pure white cannot be achieved because the pore structure does not reflect light in the way required to produce a true white finish.
